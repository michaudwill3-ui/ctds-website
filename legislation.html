Laws & Legislation

State and federal legislative updates affecting dermatology practice in Connecticut — tracked and advocated for on your behalf.

Active
CT SB 1022
Scope of Practice — Advanced Practice Providers in Dermatology
Legislation under review that would expand the scope of practice for advanced practice providers performing dermatologic procedures in Connecticut, including certain surgical and cosmetic treatments currently restricted to licensed physicians.
Status: Public Hearing — Joint Committee on Public Health Session: 2025
Pending
CT HB 6784
Step Therapy Reform — Prior Authorization Requirements
Proposed legislation to reform step therapy protocols for biologic medications in dermatology, requiring insurers to honor existing treatment regimens and streamlining prior authorization for established dermatologic biologics.
Status: Committee Review Session: 2025
Passed
CT PA 24-112
Telehealth Continuity Act — Dermatology Provisions
Signed into law in 2024, this act extended telehealth reimbursement parity for dermatology services including teledermatology consultations and store-and-forward imaging through December 2026.
Status: Signed into Law — 2024 Effective: January 1, 2025
Monitor
CT SB 892
Pharmacy Benefit Manager Transparency Act
Legislation requiring greater transparency from pharmacy benefit managers regarding formulary decisions and drug pricing, with potential impact on dermatologic biologic and specialty drug access for Connecticut patients.
Status: Referred to Commerce Committee Session: 2025
Active
CMS Final Rule 2025
Physician Fee Schedule — Dermatology Impact
The 2025 CMS Physician Fee Schedule final rule includes reimbursement adjustments affecting dermatology procedure codes. The CTDS and AAD are tracking implementation and advocating for equitable reimbursement for dermatologists.
Effective: January 1, 2025 Source: CMS.gov
Pending
FDA Guidance 2025
Topical Biologics & Biosimilars — Updated Regulatory Framework
New FDA draft guidance on interchangeability standards for topical biologics and biosimilars in dermatology, including dupilumab and IL-17 inhibitor biosimilars entering the market in 2025.
Status: Public Comment Period Open Source: FDA.gov
Active
S. 2440
GOLD Card Act — Prior Authorization Reform
Federal legislation that would exempt physicians who meet quality benchmarks from burdensome prior authorization requirements under Medicare Advantage, with significant implications for dermatology practices nationwide.
Status: Senate Finance Committee Session: 119th Congress
Apr 2025
Connecticut
SB 1022 Public Hearing — Society Testimony Submitted
The CTDS submitted written testimony opposing scope-of-practice expansion for non-physician providers performing dermatologic surgical procedures. Testimony emphasized patient safety and the complexity of dermatologic diagnosis and treatment.
Mar 2025
Federal
AAD Advocacy Day — CT Delegation Participates
Connecticut dermatologists joined AAD's annual advocacy day in Washington, meeting with congressional delegations to discuss prior authorization reform, scope of practice, and Medicare reimbursement.
AAD Advocacy Resources →
Jan 2025
CMS
2025 Physician Fee Schedule Takes Effect
The 2025 CMS Physician Fee Schedule final rule went into effect January 1. Dermatology-specific code adjustments include changes to E&M visit coding, Mohs surgery reimbursement, and phototherapy procedure valuations.
CMS Final Rule →
Dec 2024
FDA
FDA Approves New IL-13 Inhibitor for Atopic Dermatitis
The FDA approved a new interleukin-13 inhibitor biologic for moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis in adults, expanding the therapeutic toolkit available to Connecticut dermatologists.
Nov 2024
Connecticut
Telehealth Parity Extended Through 2026
CT PA 24-112 extended telehealth reimbursement parity for dermatology services including teledermatology and store-and-forward imaging consultations through December 31, 2026.
